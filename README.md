## Overview

The Futakuchi-onna (二口女, 'two-mouthed woman') is a Japanese yokai featuring a woman with two mouths — a normal one on her face and a second one on the back of the head, hidden beneath the hair. The second mouth is fully functional, with lips, teeth, and a tongue. The creature's hair moves independently like a pair of serpents, allowing it to manipulate food and feed the back mouth. If the second mouth is not fed, it causes the woman tremendous pain and screams constantly — mumbling spiteful and threatening things. The condition arises through a curse, with three primary origin narratives: (1) A wife of a miser eats little and develops a second mouth that demands food, consuming double what a normal person would eat; (2) A stingy woman struck in the head by her husband's axe while chopping wood develops an unhealable wound that transforms into the second mouth; (3) A stepmother or step-sibling who starves a child to death is possessed by the victim's spirit, which lodges in the body seeking revenge through the second mouth. The futakuchi-onna belongs to the same yokai category as rokurokubi, kuchisake-onna, and yama-uba — women afflicted with a curse or supernatural disease. It appears in the Ehon Hyaku Monogatari, an illustrated collection of ghost stories.

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Vengeful spirit possession** _(Japanese folk tradition)_

The spirit of a child or stepchild who was starved to death possesses the body of the cruel person responsible, creating the second mouth as an instrument of revenge that forces the host to consume food obsessively.

### Skeptical

**Moral allegory encoding domestic anxieties** _(Folklorists studying Japanese yokai traditions)_

The futakuchi-onna encodes anxieties about domestic miserliness, cruelty to stepchildren, and the consequences of greed, expressed through a body-horror metaphor in which hidden gluttony or hidden cruelty is literally exposed.
