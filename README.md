## Overview

**Futakuchi-onna** (二口女, "two-mouthed woman") is a Japanese yokai depicting a woman with a normal appearance from the front but possessing a **second mouth on the back of her skull**, hidden beneath her hair.

This second mouth has teeth, lips, and the ability to speak—often **screaming demands for food**. The hair around the mouth can move independently like tentacles, enabling the mouth to feed itself. If not fed, the second mouth causes excruciating pain and screams constantly.

The condition typically arises as a **curse**, most commonly in stories of a stingy stepmother who starved her stepchild to death. The child's vengeful spirit creates the second mouth that consumes **twice as much food**, punishing the stepmother's miserliness with the very hunger she inflicted.

The yokai embodies themes of hidden hunger, punishment for cruelty, and domestic horror.

## Etymology & Names

### Primary Name
- **Futakuchi-onna** (二口女) — From Japanese 'futa' (二, two), 'kuchi' (口, mouth), and 'onna' (女, woman). Literally "two-mouthed woman."

## Characteristics

### Physical Description

**Front View**: Normal human woman, often described as attractive or ordinary in appearance. No visible abnormality.

**Back View**: Second fully-formed mouth located on the back of the skull, hidden under hair.

| Feature | Description | Frequency |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Primary face** | Normal human appearance | Always |
| **Second mouth** | Fully formed mouth on back of head with teeth, lips, tongue | Always |
| **Animated hair** | Hair around second mouth moves like tentacles or snakes | Common |
| **Skull opening** | Actual opening in skull where second mouth exists | Always |

### Abilities & Powers

| Ability | Description | Evidence Level |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Hair manipulation** | Hair acts as prehensile tentacles to grasp food and feed the second mouth | Folklore |
| **Second mouth speech** | Back mouth speaks independently, demands food, screams | Folklore |
| **Increased appetite** | Must consume double normal food intake | Folklore |
| **Threats and curses** | Second mouth torments the woman emotionally | Folklore |

### Behavior Patterns

#### Second Mouth Behavior
- Constantly demands food
- Screams and shrieks when hungry
- Threatens the woman if not fed
- Uses hair tentacles to snatch food independently
- Causes intense pain (skull-splitting headaches)

#### Woman's Response
- Attempts to hide the second mouth under hair
- Must acquire twice the normal amount of food
- Suffers constant torment
- Often becomes isolated due to the curse
- Lives in shame and horror

## Origins of the Curse

### Most Common Version: The Starved Stepchild

**Story**: A poor man remarries a woman who is extremely stingy and resents having to feed his child from his first marriage. To save money, she feeds the stepchild as little as possible or not at all, while eating normally herself. The child eventually dies of starvation.

**Curse**: The dead child's vengeful spirit creates a second mouth on the back of the stepmother's skull. This mouth eats ravenously, consuming twice the normal amount of food—more than the stepmother saved by starving the child.

**Punishment**: The stepmother is punished by the very hunger she inflicted, forced to endure constant demands for food and public shame.

### Alternative Origin Stories

| Version | Description | Moral Lesson |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Miserly woman** | Woman who never fed guests or the poor is cursed | Punishment for lack of generosity |
| **Yokai birth** | Some versions present futakuchi-onna as born yokai | Natural supernatural occurrence |
| **Secret eating** | Woman who ate secretly or gluttonously is cursed | Punishment for deception or gluttony |

### Curse Mechanics

- **Trigger**: Cruelty (especially to children), stinginess, gluttony, or deception
- **Manifestation**: Second mouth appears on back of skull
- **Permanence**: Usually permanent; rarely described as removable
- **Potential removal**: Atonement, exorcism (rarely successful in stories)

## Symbolism & Themes

### Core Themes

1. **Hidden Hunger**
   The second mouth represents literal and metaphorical hidden hunger—desire, need, or cruelty concealed beneath normal appearance

2. **Punishment Fits the Crime**
   Starving a child → cursed with endless hunger
   Stinginess → forced to consume twice as much

3. **Stepfamily Tensions**
   Reflects historical anxieties about stepfamilies and treatment of stepchildren in feudal Japan

4. **Appearance vs. Reality**
   Normal appearance conceals horrifying truth—what's hidden beneath

5. **Female Domestic Suffering**
   Woman trapped by supernatural affliction in domestic sphere

### Moral Lessons

- **Feed the hungry**, especially children in your care
- **Generosity is a virtue**; stinginess brings suffering
- **Cruelty to stepchildren** brings supernatural punishment
- **Hidden evil will be revealed** in monstrous form

## Cultural Context

### Edo Period Society

Futakuchi-onna stories emerged during the Edo period, reflecting:
- **Economic pressures** on families
- **Stepfamily dynamics** in a society with high remarriage rates
- **Importance of food** in a society where famine was a real threat
- **Women's domestic roles** and associated anxieties

### Kaidan Tradition

Part of **kaidan** (怪談, ghost stories) tradition:
- Cautionary tales told to reinforce social values
- Entertainment with moral lessons
- Stories shared orally and in written collections

## Modern Interpretations

### Contemporary Analysis

**Feminist Readings**: Modern scholars see themes of:
- Female hunger (literal and for agency)
- Suppression of women's voices
- Domestic oppression made literal
- Split between public appearance and private suffering

**Psychological Horror**: Emphasis on:
- Body horror elements
- Loss of bodily autonomy
- Constant torment from within
- Inability to escape oneself
